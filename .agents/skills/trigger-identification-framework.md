# trigger-identification-framework

Use this skill to categorize buying signals into a structured taxonomy. Not all intent data is created equal.

## The 4 Primary Categories of Triggers

### 1. Inbound Triggers (Direct Intent)
The prospect is directly interacting with your brand, showing acute awareness and interest.
- **High Intent:** Demo Request, Pricing Page Visit (Deanonymized), Contact Us form.
- **Medium Intent:** Webinar Attendance, High-Value Content Download (eBook/Report).
- **Low Intent:** Liked a social media post, subscribed to the newsletter.
- *Action:* Route immediately to Sales based on SLA.

### 2. Bridgebound Triggers (Relationship / History)
The prospect has prior context with your team, product, or ecosystem. Highly converting.
- **Closed-Lost Revival:** A deal that was lost to "timing/budget" 6+ months ago.
- **Past Champion Move:** A power user at a past customer takes a new job at an ICP target account ($$$).
- **Executive Connection:** An executive at your company is connected to an executive at the target account.

### 3. Outbound Triggers (Market Signals)
External events indicating a newly created pain point or budget allocation.
- **Firmographic Events:** Funding rounds, M&A activity, IPO filings, office expansions.
- **Personnel Events:** Hiring sprees in a specific department, replacing a key executive (e.g., New CMO hired).
- **Technographic Events:** Installing a complementary tool, or a contract expiring with a known competitor.

### 4. 3rd-Party Intent (The Noise)
Platform-level signals suggesting an account is researching software in your category (e.g., Bombora, 6sense, G2).
- **The Risk:** It only tells you the *company* is looking, not *who* at the company is looking.
- **The Rule:** Never write copy saying "I saw you were researching X." Use this data purely to prioritize which accounts your SDRs target manually.

## Output Contract
When asked to categorize or identify a trigger, provide:
- The category (Inbound, Bridgebound, Outbound, 3rd-Party).
- The definition of the exact event.
- An assessment of whether it is a "Company Level" vs "Persona Level" trigger.
